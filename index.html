I'm a Master of Urban Planning student at Pratt Institute's School of Architecture, where studying planning within a design school — and an art and design school at that — has given me a strong grounding in urban design alongside the analytical and policy side of the field. My focus is on housing policy, municipal finance, and land use — using tools like Python, GeoPandas, and ArcGIS to ask questions about affordability, development patterns, and how cities allocate resources. I'm particularly interested in transit oriented development, downtown revitalization, and the craft of physical planning: how master plans shape the long arc of a city's growth and what it takes to translate policy into built form. I'm drawn to work that lives at the boundary between rigorous analysis and spatial design: parcel-level data, geospatial visualization, policy research that actually has something to say. I believe good data and good design can make planning more legible — and more equitable. When I'm not deep in a dataset, I'm thinking about architecture, sketching, or riding bikes on gravel roads that probably weren't meant for bikes.

East 12th Street Section View: Avenue A to Avenue B

As part of my studies, I developed a section view of a street in the East Village for a potential redesign of street use. Beginning with a hand-drawn section view of the study area, I then digitized it with InDesign and made a 3D model of the surrounding area with SketchUp.

Vacant Lots Within a 0.5 Mile Walkshed and 0.5 Mile Euclidean Buffer of Subway Stations

GIS analysis mapping vacant development lots relative to subway station walksheds across Broad Channel and the Rockaway Peninsula, produced at Pratt GCPE.

School Fiscal Impacts of Shiodome College Redevelopment, Greenlee, NY

Fiscal impact analysis projecting $1.24M net positive impact on the Greenlee Central School District from a 72-unit mixed housing development. Prepared for Stanley County Citizens for Social Responsibility, November 2025.
